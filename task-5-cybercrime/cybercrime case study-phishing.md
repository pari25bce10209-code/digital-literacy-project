## Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack on Facebook and Google (2013–2015)

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers pretend to be a trusted person or company to trick others into sharing sensitive information or sending money. A real example of this happened between 2013 and 2015, when Facebook and Google were scammed out of over $100 million.

In this case, the attacker pretended to be Quanta Computer, a real hardware supplier for both companies. They sent fake emails along with invoices that looked genuine. These emails were carefully designed to appear like normal business communication, which made them difficult to detect. Employees in the finance departments received these emails and, believing them to be real, processed the payments. As a result, large amounts of money were transferred to the attacker’s bank accounts.

The main targets were employees responsible for handling payments and financial transactions. This shows that even experienced professionals can fall victim if the attack looks convincing enough. The consequences included huge financial losses and also affected the companies’ internal security trust.

This incident highlights the importance of verifying payment requests, checking sender details carefully, and not relying only on email communication for important transactions.
